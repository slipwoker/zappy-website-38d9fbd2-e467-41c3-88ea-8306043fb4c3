Acquiring Original Work

Nicole believes that acquiring art should feel personal and unhurried. Each piece is priced according to its size, medium, and the time invested in the studio, with complete transparency from the very first conversation. Whether you are drawn to a finished gallery piece or are curious about commissioning something entirely new, Nicole welcomes your inquiry. Availability changes with the seasons, and she is always happy to share what is currently on the easel.

Below you will find a general guide to pricing ranges for original paintings. For exact pricing on a specific work, or to discuss a custom commission, please reach out directly. Nicole looks forward to connecting with you.

The Collector's Experience

Original Artwork & Authenticity

Every painting is an original work, signed and accompanied by a certificate of authenticity. I document the materials, palette, and story behind each piece so your collection carries a complete provenance from studio to home. This is more than a purchase — it is the beginning of a relationship with the artwork.

Care & Preservation Guidance

Oil paintings reward thoughtful stewardship, and I want your piece to age beautifully for generations. With every acquisition, I share detailed care recommendations — from ideal lighting and humidity levels to gentle cleaning — so you can feel confident displaying your painting in a cherished space within your home.

Commission Collaboration

A commissioned painting is a deeply personal endeavor. I work closely with you from initial concept sketches through final varnish, ensuring the scale, palette, and emotional tone align with your vision. Clear timelines, transparent pricing, and gentle communication make the process as rewarding as the finished canvas.

Collecting & Commissioning

Answers to the most common questions about acquiring, caring for, and commissioning original artwork. If you don't find what you're looking for, please reach out directly—I'm always happy to talk about the process.

How do I reserve a painting I'm interested in?

Once you've found a piece you love, simply get in touch via the contact form or email. I can place a 48-hour courtesy hold while we finalize details. To secure the work, a 30% deposit is required, with the remaining balance due prior to shipping or collection.

What does the commission process look like?

Commissions begin with a conversation about size, palette, and the feeling you'd like the piece to convey. After we agree on a direction, I provide a sketch and a timeline. A 40% non-refundable deposit initiates the work, with the balance settled upon your approval of the finished painting.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes, I ship to collectors throughout the United States, Canada, the UK, and the European Union. Each painting is carefully packed in a custom crate with full insurance coverage. Shipping costs are calculated based on size and destination and will be quoted at the time of purchase.

Are framing options available?

Most works on canvas arrive ready to hang with gallery-wrapped edges, so framing is optional. For works on paper, I collaborate with a fine arts framer who offers a curated selection of hand-finished mouldings. I'm pleased to guide you through those choices.

How should I care for an original painting?

Display your artwork away from direct sunlight, heat sources, and areas of high humidity. Dust gently with a soft, dry microfiber cloth—never use cleaning products. With proper placement and occasional light dusting, your painting will remain vibrant for generations.

What is your typical timeline for a new work?

Available studio pieces ship within 5–7 business days. Commissioned paintings generally require 4–8 weeks depending on scale and complexity. I'll provide a firm delivery window once we've defined the scope of your project, and I keep collectors updated with progress images along the way.
