The Studio Process

Materials, mark making, and the quiet rhythm of the studio.

Sketching & Composition

Every painting begins with a quiet conversation between pencil and paper. Nicole works in natural light, allowing the shifting shadows to guide her initial marks. These early sketches are not rigid blueprints but fluid explorations of balance, movement, and emotional weight.

Layering & Pigment

Using a restrained palette of earth tones and muted hues, Nicole builds her surfaces slowly. Thin washes give way to thicker impasto passages, creating a tactile depth that invites the viewer to look closer. Each layer holds the memory of the one beneath it.

Texture & Final Details

The final stage is a meditation on texture. Nicole sands, scrapes, and reworks the surface until it feels alive. The finished painting carries the evidence of its making — subtle ridges, soft patinas, and a quiet luminosity that changes with the light in the room.

Availability & Inquiries

A practical guide for collectors interested in acquiring original work or commissioning a new piece.

Current Inventory

Most paintings shown in the gallery are available for immediate purchase unless marked as sold. I update the collection regularly, but please reach out if you have your eye on a specific piece — I’ll confirm its status and send you detailed images, dimensions, and pricing within 24 hours.

For collectors visiting the fine arts center exhibitions, I can also arrange private viewings of works not currently displayed online.

Commissions & Framing

I welcome a limited number of commissions each season. Lead times typically range from six to ten weeks depending on scale and complexity. We’ll discuss your vision, preferred palette, and dimensions before I begin.

All paintings are delivered ready to hang with hand-selected, archival-quality framing. I personally oversee every step from studio to crate to ensure your artwork arrives safely and beautifully presented.
